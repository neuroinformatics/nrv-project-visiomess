Two types of bimodal direction tuning

Component cell Pattern cell
(1-D velocity detection) (2-D velocity detection)
Moving stimulus activating a component cell
  • Bar at optimal speed
  • Spot at optimal speed
  • Spot at higher speed
  • Spot at more higher speed
  • Shift of optimal direction at each speed for a spot
 Moving stimulus activating a pattern cell
  • Spot at optimal speed
  • Bar at optimal speed
  • Bar at lower speed
  • Bar at more lower speed
  • Shift of optimal direction at each speed for a bar
  
Bimodal direction tuning appears at higher speed than optimal for moving spot stimulus. This property reflects the characteristics of Hough transformation. Bimodal direction tuning appears at lower speed than optimal for moving bar stimulus. This property reflects the characteristics of inverse Hough transformation.
