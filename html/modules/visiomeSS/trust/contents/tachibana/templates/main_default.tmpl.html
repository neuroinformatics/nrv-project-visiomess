Membrane current of ON-transient amacrine cells in the mouse retina

Matsui K, Hasegawa J, Tachibana M

The Microsoft Excel files contains data appeared in Matsui et al (2001).

In this paper, the membrane current of ON-transient amacrine cells in the mouse retinal preparation was recorded under the whole-cell clamp, and it was demonstrated that GABA-C receptors at the terminal of bipolar cells modulate the amount of glutamate release. The data include A) time, B) the timing of light stimulation, C) the membrane current recorded from ON-transient type amacrine cells.

Four data sets were obtained in the following conditions:

  1. control (Fig. 3C_1),
  2. blockade of NMDA receptors by D-AP5 (Fig. 3C_2),
  3. blockde of GABA-C receptors by TPMPA (Fig. 9A_1), and
  4. effects of D-AP5 and TPMPA (Fig. 9A_2).

The slice preparation was always superfused with the Mg-free solution containing picrotoxin, strychnine, D-tubocrarine and atropine.
